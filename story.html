Looking Back - The Incident that got me really thinking about Disability.

The most memorable experience of my life was the incident that happened some nine years ago.

December 8, 2022. by Lamin M.K Suwareh

One beautiful Afternoon, my mom came to my room and informed me that one of my uncles who lives in Gunjur - south Gambia has called me to go and participate in the national polio immunization days that was about to tee-off. She told me that my uncle registered his son to participate for the vaccination but unfortunately he was sitting an examination that is why he wanted me to go replace him. I made no objection to that since I was not going to school and also been jobless. That very evening, I packed my bag and went to my uncle home in Gunjur and spent the night there.

The following morning I bathed and went to Gunjur health center to attend the workshop. Later the morning, the workshop began. The organizers and the nurses taught us how to carry out the job to vaccinate children and take records. After they were done, they divided us into groups of two each to perform the job the following two days. I was paired with a lady who was about five years older than I was. We exchanged some pleasantries but after she realized that I was deaf in ears, she left me there and went inside the hospital and informed a staff whom she knew that she wouldn’t pair with me.

The staff then came and tried to pair me with other participants especially the boys, but they all refused. There was a lot of brouhaha. Some participants were making fun of me and saying to each other that I went there because of money. After a shot discussion among them, I was told to go home, thus disqualified. I handed back the t-shirt given to me to wear during the vaccination period, but I was told to take it.

Amid the suck, I stood up, ready to go home. There was contempt focus on my attention. I thought my legs wouldn’t perform there functions but they did no such thing. Had I been aware of that disgrace, I would have simply not accepted my uncle's request let alone bother myself going there. Been a disabled person, I was the talk of the day, almost every tom, dick and harry have something to say about me. I left the hospital campus and walked about two hundred meters back home without looking back.

On reaching home!

On reaching home, I already meet my uncle there. He told me he was called to answer at the hospital and thus closed work before his closing time. He advised me to not over think about the incident that took place and also gave me some words of encouragement. My aunty on the other hand reaction was different to my uncle’s. She put all the blames on the staffs and my colleagues. She complained that I should be allowed to participate by force. That evening I left for home. I met my mom who already knew everything even before my arrival. She responded to my plights by giving me more words of encouragement.
This was one of the incidents that make me thick and never try to give up even for a second. And I will never give up Inn Shaa Allah!
